Installing or replacing a toilet may seem easy when someone else does it, especially if they have experience. However, it can be more complicated when you have to do it yourself. Improper installation can cause the toilet to break down sooner than expected. In addition to minor issues, people may have to deal with:

  • Overtightened or loose nuts and bolts. Finding the perfect balance is key when tightening nuts and bolts during toilet installation. Overtightening them can put unnecessary pressure on the porcelain, potentially causing it to chip or crack. If the toilet base is not screwed in tightly, it can cause your toilet to wobble and become unstable. The instability can damage the wax ring underneath and cause leakage.
  • Leaks. When the wax ring isn’t properly sealed, it can leak wastewater from the bottom. Such leaks can lead to mould on the floor. Water can also leak through cracks or loose connections.
